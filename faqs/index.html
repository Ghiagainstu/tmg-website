Everything you need to know about advertising on Baidu & Xiaohongshu

Straight answers from a China media agency. No fluff. If you do not see your question here, reach out directly.

Account & Setup 4 questions
B1 Can I open a Baidu account without a Chinese domestic company?

Yes. Baidu supports account opening for foreign companies and entities. You do not need a Chinese domestic business license to get started — we handle the process for you.

B2 Is there a difference between foreign and Chinese domestic Baidu accounts?

No functional difference. The only distinction is in the ad display: the company name shown at the bottom of your ads will be either your foreign company name or your Chinese company name, depending on which entity the account is registered under.

B3 What documents do I need to open a Baidu account?

Required documents are:

(a) Business license copy + certified translation (stamped with company seal);

(b) Enterprise info screenshot from government website + certified translation (stamped with company seal);

(c) Bank account certificate + translated document from designated institution (stamped with company seal), such as bank transfer records, bank statements, local tax certificates, checks, or monthly statements;

(d, optional) Screenshot of landing page homepage (stamped with company seal).

If you have difficulty providing these documents, we are happy to assist.

B4 What should I do if my Baidu ad fails review?

Follow these steps: (1) Read the review notes carefully; (2) Revise the specific content flagged; (3) Re-submit for review; (4) If you believe the rejection is incorrect, file an appeal for special review. We handle this process for our managed clients.

Fees & Budget 3 questions
B5 Are there fees besides the advertising budget for Baidu?

Yes. There is an annual account authentication fee of CNY 600. For new users, this fee is refunded to your ad account after the first year — effectively making it free for the first year.

B6 What is the minimum top-up amount for Baidu?

Baidu\'s official rule is a minimum top-up of CNY 2,400. However, to save on transaction fees and reduce transfer time, we recommend a single top-up of CNY 10,000 or more.

B7 How much does it cost to open a Baidu account?

Pre-paid advertising fee: minimum CNY 2,400. Service fee: starting from USD 100, which includes document translation, document review, and submission handling. If you have difficulty providing the required documents, we are happy to assist.

Billing & CPC 3 questions
B8 Does Baidu support post-payment (pay later)?

No. Baidu only supports pre-payment. You must have sufficient balance in your account before your ads can run. There is no credit or post-pay option.

B9 How does Baidu advertising charge?

Cost-per-click (CPC): You are charged only when a user clicks your ad. The approximate formula is: your cost per click = (competitor\'s bid x competitor\'s quality score / your quality score) + CNY 0.01. You can also use oCPC (optimized CPC), where you pay for completed conversions rather than clicks.

B10 How much does each click cost on Baidu? What is the minimum monthly budget?

Cost per click: There is no uniform price. Low-competition keywords may cost a few yuan per click. In highly competitive industries, popular keywords can cost dozens to over CNY 100 per click.

Minimum monthly budget: the minimum daily budget is CNY 50, which means the theoretical monthly minimum is CNY 1,500. The actual cost depends on search volume for your target keywords. There is no upper cap.

Compliance & Review 4 questions
B11 Can I run ads if my Baidu account shows Failed Review?

No. Ads cannot run until your account passes review. You must resolve the rejection issues and get approved before any ads go live.

B12 What are the common reasons for Baidu ad rejection?

The most common rejection reasons are: (1) Expired qualifications or licenses; (2) Content contains prohibited words; (3) Website is unreachable or offline; (4) Content is false, exaggerated, or misleading. Any other compliance violations can also lead to rejection.

B13 What are common prohibited words in Baidu ads?

Common prohibited words include absolute claims such as: cheapest, number one in the country, 100% effective, guaranteed results, and similar superlative or unverifiable claims. Use Baidu\'s prohibited word detection tool and refer to China\'s Advertising Law for self-inspection before submitting.

B14 Do healthcare and finance industries have special requirements on Baidu?

Yes. Regulated industries such as healthcare and finance must provide industry-specific licenses, avoid efficacy claims in all ad copy and landing page content, and ensure all claims are substantiated and compliant with Chinese advertising regulations.

Account & Setup 4 questions
X1 Can foreign companies run ads on Xiaohongshu?

Yes, but the process is not as straightforward as Baidu. Xiaohongshu requires a Chinese business license for direct account registration. Foreign companies typically work through a local agency partner who handles the account setup, qualification filing, and creative submission on your behalf.

Companies registered in Hong Kong, Japan, South Korea, or Taiwan can apply with additional documentation. For all other countries, eligibility is assessed on a case-by-case basis — contact us to check before you start.

X2 What documents are needed to advertise on Xiaohongshu?

At minimum, you need a Chinese business license (if registering directly) or a partnership agreement with a certified agency. Depending on your industry, you may also need:

(a) Trademark registration certificate + authorization letter (both required for non-owned trademarks);

(b) ICP filing screenshot (if your ad mentions a website);

(c) Software copyright certificate (if it references an app).

Foreign entities need to provide a BR or CR certificate (Hong Kong) or a declaration letter (Japan, South Korea, Taiwan).

X3 How long does Xiaohongshu account approval take?

The review process on Xiaohongshu\'s professional account takes up to 30 days. The platform charges a one-time review fee of CNY 600 per application — this is non-refundable even if the review fails. Because of this cost and timeline, it is worth getting the paperwork right the first time.

X4 What are the rules for the Xiaohongshu account name?

The account name must relate to your registered business name, trademark, or website/app name. It cannot be vague, impersonate real people, or mimic Xiaohongshu itself. Keep it under 12 Chinese characters. Absolute claims like "No. 1," "top-tier," or "best in the country" are prohibited under China\'s Advertising Law and will cause rejection. Pinyin used to replace Chinese characters is not supported.

Fees & Budget 3 questions
X5 How does Xiaohongshu advertising charge?

Two options: CPC (cost per click) and CPM (cost per thousand impressions). You pick the model that fits your goal. CPC makes sense when you want actual engagement. CPM works when brand exposure matters more. The platform is pre-paid only — no credit terms.

X6 What is the minimum budget to advertise on Xiaohongshu?

There is no hard minimum published by the platform, but in practice you need at least a few thousand CNY to test and gather meaningful data. Once your cumulative spend exceeds CNY 3,000, you become eligible for private messaging automation, which is worth having if lead generation is your goal.

X7 Are there any fees besides the advertising spend on Xiaohongshu?

Yes. The professional account review fee is CNY 600 (one-time, non-refundable). If you work through an agency, there is typically a service fee on top of media spend. Ask us for a quote — it depends on the scope of work.

Billing & CPC 3 questions
X8 How much does a click cost on Xiaohongshu?

It varies a lot by industry and targeting. Low-competition niches might see CPCs of a few yuan. Competitive categories like beauty, fashion, or mother and baby can go much higher. There is no fixed price — the system runs an auction, and your cost depends on competing bids and ad quality.

X9 Why is my Xiaohongshu ad spend not going through?

Three things to check: your bid might be too low (raise it), your targeting might be too narrow (loosen it), or your creative might have failed review (check the ad status). These are the most common reasons campaigns stall on Xiaohongshu.

X10 I am getting impressions but no conversions on Xiaohongshu. What is wrong?

Impressions without conversions usually point to an audience mismatch or a landing page problem. Either you are reaching people who are not your real customers, or the page they land on does not convince them to act. Tightening targeting and rewriting the landing page copy are the first things to try.

Compliance & Review 5 questions
X11 What gets ads rejected on Xiaohongshu?

The big ones: using someone\'s face in your avatar (not allowed), mentioning prohibited industries (finance, gambling, tobacco, medical/health, dietary supplements, weight loss), and including exaggerated or unverifiable claims. Content that looks copied or that feels like a hard-sell rather than genuine content also gets flagged. Xiaohongshu\'s reviewers are strict about maintaining the platform\'s authentic feel.

X12 Are there industries that cannot advertise on Xiaohongshu?

Yes. Financial services (securities, private equity, cryptocurrency, P2P lending), gambling, tobacco, and certain health/medical categories are either restricted or completely banned. Prohibited categories also include military/police related imagery, superstition content, online fraud/job scams, micro-business/reselling, gaming account trading, and certain regulated professions.

Some industries need special approval before they can run ads. Check with us before you plan your campaign — it saves time.

X13 What should I know about trademark use in Xiaohongshu ads?

If the account name references a trademark, you need to prove you own it or have authorization. Own it? A registration certificate, electronic copy, or acceptance notice works. Someone else\'s trademark? You need both a formal trademark registration certificate and an authorization letter valid for at least one year from the submission date. Make sure the certificate is not expired, withdrawn, or rejected — invalid trademarks will cause the whole application to fail.

X14 My Xiaohongshu ad mentions a website. Do I need an ICP filing?

Yes. If your account name or ad content references a website, Xiaohongshu requires a screenshot of the ICP filing (MIIT) from China\'s MIIT. One hard rule: Xiaohongshu does not accept ICP filings for websites hosted outside mainland China. If your site is on an overseas server, your application will be rejected — you need a China-hosted landing page to proceed.

X15 What are the rules for the Xiaohongshu account avatar?

The avatar cannot be a real person\'s face or a photo showing someone\'s face. It must be relevant to your brand, product, or business entity. Using another company\'s registered trademark as your avatar is also not allowed — if it looks like it belongs to someone else, it will be rejected.

The avatar must comply with Chinese laws: no political, violent, or discriminatory content, and no imagery associated with prohibited industries. Once the review passes, you cannot reapply to change the avatar for 7 days.
