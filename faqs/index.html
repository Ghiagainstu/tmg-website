Account & Setup 4 questions
B1 Can I open a Baidu account without a Chinese domestic company?

Yes. Baidu supports account opening for foreign companies and entities. You do not need a Chinese domestic business license to get started — we handle the process for you.

B2 Is there a difference between foreign and Chinese domestic Baidu accounts?

No functional difference. The only distinction is in the ad display: the company name shown at the bottom of your ads will be either your foreign company name or your Chinese company name, depending on which entity the account is registered under.

B3 What documents do I need to open a Baidu account?

Required documents are:

(a) Business license copy + certified translation (stamped with company seal);

(b) Enterprise info screenshot from government website + certified translation (stamped with company seal);

(c) Bank account certificate + translated document from designated institution (stamped with company seal), such as bank transfer records, bank statements, local tax certificates, checks, or monthly statements;

(d, optional) Screenshot of landing page homepage (stamped with company seal).

If you have difficulty providing these documents, we are happy to assist.

B4 What should I do if my Baidu ad fails review?

Follow these steps: (1) Read the review notes carefully; (2) Revise the specific content flagged; (3) Re-submit for review; (4) If you believe the rejection is incorrect, file an appeal for special review. We handle this process for our managed clients.

Fees & Budget 3 questions
B5 Are there fees besides the advertising budget for Baidu?

Yes. There is an annual account authentication fee of CNY 600. For new users, this fee is refunded to your ad account after the first year — effectively making it free for the first year.

B6 What is the minimum top-up amount for Baidu?

Baidu\'s official rule is a minimum top-up of CNY 2,400. However, to save on transaction fees and reduce transfer time, we recommend a single top-up of CNY 10,000 or more.

B7 How much does it cost to open a Baidu account?

Pre-paid advertising fee: minimum CNY 2,400. Service fee: starting from USD 100, which includes document translation, document review, and submission handling. If you have difficulty providing the required documents, we are happy to assist.

Billing & CPC 3 questions
B8 Does Baidu support post-payment (pay later)?

No. Baidu only supports pre-payment. You must have sufficient balance in your account before your ads can run. There is no credit or post-pay option.

B9 How does Baidu advertising charge?

Cost-per-click (CPC): You are charged only when a user clicks your ad. The approximate formula is: your cost per click = (competitor\'s bid x competitor\'s quality score / your quality score) + CNY 0.01. You can also use oCPC (optimized CPC), where you pay for completed conversions rather than clicks.

B10 How much does each click cost on Baidu? What is the minimum monthly budget?

Cost per click: There is no uniform price. Low-competition keywords may cost a few yuan per click. In highly competitive industries, popular keywords can cost dozens to over CNY 100 per click.

Minimum monthly budget: the minimum daily budget is CNY 50, which means the theoretical monthly minimum is CNY 1,500. The actual cost depends on search volume for your target keywords. There is no upper cap.

Compliance & Review 4 questions
B11 Can I run ads if my Baidu account shows Failed Review?

No. Ads cannot run until your account passes review. You must resolve the rejection issues and get approved before any ads go live.

B12 What are the common reasons for Baidu ad rejection?

The most common rejection reasons are: (1) Expired qualifications or licenses; (2) Content contains prohibited words; (3) Website is unreachable or offline; (4) Content is false, exaggerated, or misleading. Any other compliance violations can also lead to rejection.

B13 What are common prohibited words in Baidu ads?

Common prohibited words include absolute claims such as: cheapest, number one in the country, 100% effective, guaranteed results, and similar superlative or unverifiable claims. Use Baidu\'s prohibited word detection tool and refer to China\'s Advertising Law for self-inspection before submitting.

B14 Do healthcare and finance industries have special requirements on Baidu?

Yes. Regulated industries such as healthcare and finance must provide industry-specific licenses, avoid efficacy claims in all ad copy and landing page content, and ensure all claims are substantiated and compliant with Chinese advertising regulations.

X1 Can foreign companies run ads on Xiaohongshu?

Yes, but the process is not as straightforward as Baidu. Xiaohongshu requires a Chinese business license for direct account registration. Foreign companies typically work through a local agency partner who handles the account setup, qualification filing, and creative submission on your behalf.

Companies registered in Hong Kong, Japan, South Korea, or Taiwan can apply with additional documentation. For all other countries, eligibility is assessed on a case-by-case basis — contact us to check before you start.

X2 What documents are needed to advertise on Xiaohongshu?

At minimum, you need a Chinese business license (if registering directly) or a partnership agreement with a certified agency. Depending on your industry, you may also need:

(a) Trademark registration certificate + authorization letter (both required for non-owned trademarks);

(b) ICP filing screenshot (if your ad mentions a website);

(c) Software copyright certificate (if it references an app).

Foreign entities need to provide a BR or CR certificate (Hong Kong) or a declaration letter (Japan, South Korea, Taiwan).

X3 How long does Xiaohongshu account approval take?

The review process on Xiaohongshu\'s professional account takes up to 30 days. The platform charges a one-time review fee of CNY 600 per application — this is non-refundable even if the review fails. Because of this cost and timeline, it is worth getting the paperwork right the first time.

X4 What are the rules for the Xiaohongshu account name?

The account name must relate to your registered business name, trademark, or website/app name. It cannot be vague, impersonate real people, or mimic Xiaohongshu itself. Keep it under 12 Chinese characters. Absolute claims like "No. 1," "top-tier," or "best in the country" are prohibited under China\'s Advertising Law and will cause rejection. Pinyin used to replace Chinese characters is not supported.

X5 How does Xiaohongshu advertising charge?

Two options: CPC (cost per click) and CPM (cost per thousand impressions). You pick the model that fits your goal. CPC makes sense when you want actual engagement. CPM works when brand exposure matters more. The platform is pre-paid only — no credit terms.

X6 What is the minimum budget to advertise on Xiaohongshu?

There is no hard minimum published by the platform, but in practice you need at least a few thousand CNY to test and gather meaningful data. Once your cumulative spend exceeds CNY 3,000, you become eligible for private messaging automation, which is worth having if lead generation is your goal.

X7 Are there any fees besides the advertising spend on Xiaohongshu?

Yes. The professional account review fee is CNY 600 (one-time, non-refundable). If you work through an agency, there is typically a service fee on top of media spend. Ask us for a quote — it depends on the scope of work.

X9 Why is my Xiaohongshu ad spend not going through?

Three things to check: your bid might be too low (raise it), your targeting might be too narrow (loosen it), or your creative might have failed review (check the ad status). These are the most common reasons campaigns stall on Xiaohongshu.

Impressions without conversions usually point to an audience mismatch or a landing page problem. One hard rule: Xiaohongshu does not accept ICP filings for websites hosted outside mainland China. If your site is on an overseas server, your application will be rejected — you need a China-hosted landing page to proceed.

X15 What are the rules for the Xiaohongshu account avatar?

The avatar cannot be a real person\'s face or a photo showing someone\'s face. It must be relevant to your brand, product, or business entity. Using another company\'s registered trademark as your avatar is also not allowed — if it looks like it belongs to someone else, it will be rejected.

The avatar must comply with Chinese laws: no political, violent, or discriminatory content, and no imagery associated with prohibited industries. Once the review passes, you cannot reapply to change the avatar for 7 days.

Fees & Budget 4 questions
W3 How does WeChat advertising charge?

WeChat offers four pricing models. Choose based on your campaign goal:

CPM (Cost Per Mille) — Pay per 1,000 impressions. Works best for brand awareness campaigns where reach matters more than clicks.

CPC (Cost Per Click) — Pay only when someone clicks your ad. Better for driving traffic to your landing page, mini program, or official account.

Per Article — Used for influencer content (mutual selection ads). You pay a fixed fee per published article.

CPS (Cost Per Sale) — Pay only when a sale is made. You set the commission percentage and pay out of actual revenue generated.

Most advertisers start with CPM or CPC. The right choice depends on your campaign objective — we can advise on which model fits your goals best.

W11 What is oCPC and when should I use it?

oCPC stands for Optimized Cost Per Click. It is an intelligent bidding mode where WeChat optimizes toward your actual conversion goal rather than just clicks.

With regular CPC, you pay for each click regardless of what happens after. With oCPC, you define a conversion event (like a form submission, app install, or purchase), and WeChat's algorithm optimizes delivery toward users most likely to complete that action.

Use oCPC when you want to focus on results, not just traffic. The catch: you need enough conversion data (typically 20+ conversions per week) for the algorithm to learn effectively. If you are starting from scratch, regular CPC or CPM might be better until you have some data to feed the system.

W12 How does WeChat recharge and deduct advertising fees?

WeChat Ads runs on a pre-paid model. You deposit funds into your ad account before your ads go live. There is no post-pay or credit option for most advertisers.

Recharge is done through the Tencent Ads platform (ads.e.qq.com) or through your agency partner. Minimum recharge amounts vary — check with us for current thresholds.

Fees are deducted based on your chosen billing model (CPM, CPC, oCPC, etc.) and are calculated in real time. You can set daily or campaign-level spending caps to control your budget, which is useful when you are testing.

For foreign companies, working through a certified agency is usually the smoother option — the agency handles account funding and billing logistics in CNY.

W4 What is the minimum budget to advertise on WeChat?

There is no official minimum budget published by WeChat, but in practice you need at least CNY 5,000 to 10,000 for meaningful testing. Spending less often means not gathering enough data to optimize your campaigns effectively.

WeChat ads work well for brands that want to build awareness within specific Moments feed placements or target Official Account followers. If your goal is lead generation, there are more cost-efficient platforms — but WeChat is unmatched for reach within the WeChat ecosystem.

Billing & CPC 6 questions
W13 What do CPM, CPC, CPA, CPT mean?

Think of it as "what you are paying for":

CPM — Cost Per Mille. You pay per 1,000 impressions. 1,000 people see your ad, you get charged once.

CPC — Cost Per Click. You pay only when someone clicks. No click, no charge.

CPA — Cost Per Action. You pay when someone completes a specific action, like downloading your app or signing up. Useful when you want to pay for results, not just traffic.

CPT — Cost Per Time. You pay a fixed fee for a time slot, such as CNY 10,000 for one day. Common for reserved placements and contract deals.

oCPM / oCPC — The smart versions. The system optimizes delivery toward users most likely to convert, which means less guesswork for you. Good for beginners who want the algorithm to handle targeting.

Different ad placements and campaign goals call for different billing models. You pick based on what you are trying to achieve.

W14 What is the difference between auction ads and contract ads?

Two different ways to buy ad space:

Auction ads — Compete in real time with other advertisers. Higher bid plus better ad quality wins the slot. You control the budget yourself, can pause anytime, and there is no minimum spend. This is what most advertisers start with.

Contract ads — Negotiate a fixed price with the platform upfront for guaranteed placement. You get a fixed position at a fixed time for a fixed duration. Requires more budget and advance planning. Makes sense when you have a product launch or seasonal campaign and need guaranteed visibility.

Auction ads are simpler and more flexible. Contract ads are for when you need certainty. Most TMG clients begin with auction ads.

W5 How does ad bidding work on WeChat?

Two factors determine whether your ad gets shown: your bid and your ad quality score.

Bid — What you are willing to pay per action (click, impression, or conversion). You set this when creating your campaign.

Ad quality — Calculated from relevance, click-through rate, and landing page experience. Higher quality ads can win auctions even with lower bids.

It is not just about how much you spend. A well-crafted ad with strong relevance can outperform a competitor bidding higher. WeChat's auction system rewards relevance alongside budget.

W6 How does WeChat handle malicious clicks?

WeChat runs real-time monitoring for suspicious click activity. If the same user clicks your ad multiple times in a short period, those extra clicks are filtered out and you are not charged for them.

The platform automatically flags patterns like rapid repeated clicks, automated bots, or other suspicious behavior. WeChat will not charge you for clicks deemed fraudulent.

If you notice unusual activity in your dashboard, you can report it. Their team reviews reported cases and adjusts the billing accordingly.

W7 What is the average CPC on WeChat Ads?

There is no fixed CPC — it varies by industry, targeting, and ad placement. WeChat Moments ads tend to have higher CPCs than Mini Program or Official Account placements because of the premium placement.

Low-competition niches might see CPCs of a few yuan. Competitive categories like beauty, fashion, or food and beverage can go significantly higher. The auction-based system means your bid and ad quality both matter.

We will share benchmark data for your specific industry during our onboarding call.

oCPM / oCPC — Optimized models where you define a conversion goal and the algorithm optimizes delivery. These typically deliver better ROI once you have conversion data.

CPV (Cost Per View) — Used for video ads, charged when a user watches a defined duration of your video.

Billing is pre-paid for most advertisers. Some established accounts with significant spend history may qualify for monthly post-pay arrangements.

BI7 What are typical CPM and CPC rates on Bilibili?

Bilibili generally has lower rates than mainstream platforms because the auction is less competitive — though this also means smaller audience volume. Typical ranges:

CPM: USD 2 to 8 (CNY 15 to 55) per 1,000 impressions, depending on placement and targeting.

CPC: USD 0.30 to 1.20 (CNY 2 to 8) per click. Gaming, tech, and anime-adjacent categories tend to have higher engagement.

CPA through oCPM: USD 8 to 30 (CNY 55 to 210) depending on conversion event and audience fit. App installs and website visits are common goals; lead forms and purchases require more optimization.

These are indicative benchmarks. Actual costs depend on creative quality, audience targeting precision, and competition at the time of delivery.

BI13 Why are my Bilibili ads getting rejected?

Common rejection reasons on Bilibili:

(4) Missing or invalid ICP filing for the landing page URL;

(5) References to Bilibili's IP, mascot (2233娘), or trademarked elements without authorization;

(6) Content referencing gaming restrictions, age-gating, or other policy-sensitive topics without proper context;

(7) Content that could be perceived as exploiting youth audiences or controversial social topics.

We audit creative against Bilibili's compliance guidelines before submission to minimize rejections.

For best results, create Bilibili-specific content or significantly adapt existing creative to fit the platform's culture.

GEO Basics 3 questions
G1 What exactly is GEO?

GEO stands for Generative Engine Optimization. It is the practice of making your brand and content easier for AI systems — like DeepSeek, Doubao, Kimi, and Wenxin — to find, understand, and reference when answering user questions.

Where SEO targets search engine rankings, GEO targets AI-generated answers. When someone asks an AI a question and the AI mentions your brand in its response, that is GEO working.

G2 How is GEO different from SEO?

SEO and GEO serve different channels and produce different outcomes.

SEO puts your website higher in search engine results pages. Users still need to click through to your site.

GEO aims to get your brand mentioned directly inside an AI answer. The user may never leave the AI interface — they get a recommendation without clicking anything.

Think of SEO as earning a spot on a menu. GEO is getting the waiter to recommend your dish by name.

G3 Which AI platforms does GEO target in China?

Our GEO service focuses on the AI platforms most used by Chinese consumers:

DeepSeek — fastest-growing reasoning model in China, widely adopted across industries

Doubao (ByteDance) — integrated with Douyin, massive user base, frequently cited by younger audiences

Kimi (Moonshot) — strong with professionals and knowledge workers

Wenxin Yiyan (Baidu) — benefits from Baidu search data, relevant for users with commercial intent

We track which platforms your target audience actually uses and prioritize accordingly.

We provide monthly reports with trend charts. You should expect 3 to 6 months of data before seeing consistent patterns.

GEO moves slower than paid ads. Most clients start seeing measurable changes within 3 to 6 months of consistent optimization work.

The reason: AI systems update their models periodically. Changes we make today may take weeks or months to propagate through model retraining cycles. This is different from SEO, where algorithm updates can produce overnight shifts.

We will share early signals within the first month — internal testing results, preliminary probe checks — but the real picture takes patience.

G11 Should I do GEO or SEO first?

SEO first, in most cases. A functioning SEO foundation — a crawlable website, structured content, proper metadata — is also the foundation for good GEO. The technical requirements overlap significantly.

If you have no website or a site that is technically broken, fix that before paying for GEO. If you have decent SEO already, GEO is a natural next step to expand your visibility beyond traditional search.

G12 Can GEO replace my existing SEO efforts?

No. AI-generated answers do not replace search engine results pages — they sit alongside them. Users still search. Websites still get visited. Rankings still matter.

GEO is an additional channel, not a replacement. The clients who benefit most from GEO are the ones who already take SEO seriously and want to expand their presence into AI-generated conversations.

Cutting SEO to fund GEO would likely hurt your overall visibility in the long run.
