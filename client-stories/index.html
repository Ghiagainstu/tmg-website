How a Global CS:GO Platform Found Its Footing in China

When "No" Was Only Answer

When Farmskins first approached us, they were running into a wall that many international companies hit in China: platform rejection. As a global CS:GO skins trading platform, they had identified China as a massive, untapped market opportunity—but every major Chinese advertising platform turned them away.

Gaming-related advertising is heavily regulated in China. The platforms cited policy violations and told Farmskins that their business model simply wasn't compatible with Chinese advertising standards. No matter how they adjusted their messaging or how much budget they committed, the answer was always the same: "No."

What Actually Happened

That's when we stepped in. Unlike most agencies that would simply pass along the rejection or suggest abandoning the market, we took a different approach. We leveraged our deep understanding of Chinese platform policies, gaming regulations, and the nuances of platform approval processes.

We worked directly with platform teams, presented Farmskins' business model through a lens that aligned with Chinese gaming market expectations, and helped them navigate the complex compliance landscape. Where other agencies saw policy roadblocks, we saw pathways—ones that only become visible with years of experience and established relationships.

Six Years Later

The partnership that began with rejection has now spanned six years and counting. Farmskins didn't just get approved—they built a thriving advertising operation in China that continues to grow year after year.

What started as "impossible" became routine. Farmskins now runs ongoing campaigns across multiple Chinese platforms, accessing a market that once seemed completely closed to them. They've achieved sustained growth in China, proving that with the right partner, even the most regulated markets can be successfully navigated.

By the Numbers

The Farmskins success story wasn't luck—it was the result of our unique approach to China market entry:

  • Deep Platform Relationships: We don't just submit applications—we know the people reviewing them and understand their priorities.
  • Regulatory Expertise: Gaming advertising has complex rules in China. We know where the lines are drawn and how to operate within them.
  • Persistence & Strategy: Initial rejection doesn't mean impossibility. We found alternative pathways that others missed.
  • Long-term Partnership: Six years of ongoing support means Farmskins isn't just in the market—they're thriving in it.
  • Annual Media Spend: Farmskins invests $200K–$400K USD per year in media in the China market alone, a figure that continues to grow as their presence in the market expands.

For Other Companies Looking at China

If you're facing platform rejection or regulatory roadblocks in China, the Farmskins story shows there's often a way forward—if you have the right partner. We've helped companies in gaming, fintech, e-commerce, and other regulated industries find their path into the Chinese market.
